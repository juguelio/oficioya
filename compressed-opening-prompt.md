# PROMPT: Compressed opening — Will Baskerville and the Fairy Occupation

---

## The rule

The manuscript is complete. Everything needed is already written.

Do not write new sentences. Do not invent scenes. Do not improve the prose.

Cut what is slow. Reorder what is in the wrong place. If a later chapter contains world-building or setting detail that would strengthen an earlier chapter — take those words from there and move them forward.

Every word in the output exists somewhere in the manuscript.
The only additions permitted are joins of three words or fewer to connect two moved passages — flagged `[JOIN]`.

---

1. `skills/book-editing/SKILL.md`

3. All files in `feedback/` — HarperCollins developmental report

## The problem

The fairy encounter (inciting incident) arrives at Chapter 9. It should arrive by Chapter 4. Original Chapters 1–8 are setup. The fix is to compress those eight chapters into three using only the material already written — cutting what is slow, merging what shares a function, and mining later chapters for detail that belongs earlier.

## Step 3 — Draft the four compressed chapters

### New Chapter 1 — Arrival
Sources: original Chs 1–2

Take the arrival and house material from Chs 1–2. Cut what scores zero. What survives:
- The navigation humour — two beats (A-Z, missed exit), not three
- The elastic band — as a metaphor, not a physical object
- Gran as an absence — felt before the end of page one
- The arrival: gravel, engine off, Mum's door swinging open
- The house interior — the red carpet, the dust, the cream walls, the ghost outlines of pictures
- Mum and Will's exchange in the house
- Dad's hand on Will's knee: "Come on kid. Let's see our new home." — these words, exactly

If the author's description of any of these is stronger in a later chapter than in Ch 1–2 — use the later chapter's version. Move it forward.

Hook: end on something unresolved that pulls directly into Chapter 2.

### New Chapter 2 — The garden
Sources: original Chs 3–4

Take the room and garden material from Chs 3–4. Cut what scores zero. What survives:
- Will's room: dark blue walls, beams, window facing the forest, Gran's box taped in the corner
- Mum at the window: the forest, apple trees, swallows, the archway, Gran growing up here
- Morning: Dad's studio, the back garden, the path through the archway
- The fairy doors on the trees — the rainbow of colours, red as a postbox
- Dad's excitement and his reason for moving here
- Will's rage: "Are you telling me we left our lives... because someone nailed some little wooden doors they bought in bulk off eBay to these trees?" — these words, exactly
- Will storming back. Will alone in his room. Gran's box still taped.

If any setting detail needed here appears more fully in a later chapter — move it forward.

Hook: Will cannot stop thinking about the doors. Or Gran. Or both.

### New Chapter 3 — First day
Sources: original Chs 5–7 + Ch 8 dinner

Take the school material from Chs 5–7 and the dinner from Ch 8. Cut what scores zero. What survives:
- School: the uniform mistake, wrong end of the ground
- The pile-up at the door, Warwick
- Assembly: Mum walks to the microphone, Will recognises her
- Form class: Warwick's name mockery, Will holds his stare, does not cry
- Chips and cheese at lunch
- Walking home
- Dinner: Dad's lasagne, Mum trying, Will unable to answer, Will excusing himself to get air
- Will stepping into the garden — this is the last line of Chapter 3

Hook: Will is in the garden. Something is about to happen.

### New Chapter 4 — Something small, bright and angry
Source: original Chapter 9 — preserve it

This chapter is already well-paced. The only change: the dinner that originally opened Ch 9 is now the close of Ch 3. Remove those opening dinner lines. Everything else stays in the order and words the author wrote.

Thistle's speech is preserved exactly: "With your massive head and your stupidly big feet and your blazing sense of entitlement" — and everything before and after it in the original.
