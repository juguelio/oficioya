# SKILL: book-editing

---

## THE RULE — READ THIS FIRST

**The manuscript is complete. Nothing needs to be written. Everything needed is already there.**

The job is purely editorial:
- **Cut** — remove what is slow, redundant or scores zero
- **Reorder** — move material to where it serves the story better
- **Mine** — if a later chapter contains world-building, setting detail or character information that would strengthen an earlier chapter, take it from there and place it earlier

**Zero new sentences.** If a word does not exist somewhere in the manuscript — it does not go in the output.

The one exception: conjunctions and transitional phrases of three words or fewer needed to join two passages that have been moved together (e.g. "That morning," / "Back at the house,"). These must match the author's voice and be flagged with `[JOIN]`.

**The test:** take any sentence from the output. Find it in the original manuscript. If you cannot find it — delete it.

---

## Purpose
Edit Aileen Summers' manuscript for publication on KDP and Audible. Produce a tighter, faster version of the same story using only the material already written.

---

## The three permitted operations

### 1. Cut
Remove any scene, paragraph, sentence or phrase that scores zero on the three-question test:
1. Does it advance the plot?
2. Does it reveal character in a way not seen before in the book?
3. Does it establish world detail the reader needs at this point?

Zero yes answers → cut entirely.
One yes answer → keep only the single beat that earns its place.
Two or three yes answers → keep, trim any sub-moment that scores zero.

### 2. Reorder
Move material to where it serves the story better. A detail that appears in Chapter 6 but belongs in Chapter 2 gets moved to Chapter 2. The words do not change. The position does.

### 3. Mine
The manuscript contains all the world-building, setting detail and character information the story needs. If an earlier chapter feels thin or a setting feels unestablished — do not invent. Search the full manuscript for where the author describes that place, object or character, and bring that description forward to where it is needed.

Example: if Chapter 1 needs to establish the house but the author's best description of the house is in Chapter 3 — take it from Chapter 3 and place it in Chapter 1. The words are Aileen's. They just move.

---

## IMAGINED WORLD RULE

Every physical detail — place, object, character, sensory detail — must come from the manuscript.

If the detail exists somewhere in the manuscript → it can be used. If it needs to appear earlier than it currently does → move it there.

If the detail does not exist anywhere in the manuscript → it does not exist. Flag with `[WORLD GAP]` and note for the author.

Metaphors stay metaphors. The elastic band in Chapter 1 is a figure of speech. It is not a physical object.

---

## STRUCTURAL PACING RULE

When the story drags, the fix is structural. The inciting incident for middle-grade fiction should arrive by Chapter 3–4.

If eight chapters of setup precede the inciting incident — compress those eight chapters into three by cutting zero-scoring material and merging chapters that share a function. The words that survive are the author's own.

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Read the full manuscript
Before touching anything, read every chapter. Build a complete map of:
- Every setting and when it is first described
- Every character and when they appear
- Every object and where it is mentioned
- The inciting incident and what chapter it is in
- Every plot hole or continuity issue flagged in editorial feedback

This map is the working tool. Everything comes from here.

### Step 2 — Compression map
Produce a table before writing anything:

```
| Original chapter | Function | Decision | New chapter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ch X | | Cut / Keep / Compress / Merge with Ch Y | |

BEATS THAT SURVIVE (with source):
- [beat] — original Ch [X], para [Y]

BEATS THAT ARE CUT:
- [beat] — reason

MATERIAL MOVED FORWARD:
- [detail] — moved from Ch [X] to Ch [Y] — reason

PLOT HOLES FIXED:
- [issue] — fix using: [source chapter and passage]

WORLD GAPS (details not found anywhere in manuscript):
- [gap] — needs author input
```

### Step 3 — Edit
Cut, reorder, mine. Join moved passages with minimal connective tissue (`[JOIN]` flagged). Do not write new content.

### Step 4 — Voice check
Read the output. Does it sound like Aileen Summers wrote every word? It should — because she did. If any passage sounds different, smoother or more generic — it has been rewritten. Find the original and restore it.

---

## Output format

```
COMPRESSION MAP
[table]

---

CHAPTER DRAFTS
[edited chapters — the author's own words, cut and reordered]

---

AUTHOR NOTES
[WORLD GAPs — details not found in the manuscript that the story needs]
[JOIN flags — transitional phrases added]
[open questions for the author]
```

---

## Final checklist

- [ ] Every sentence found in the original manuscript (or flagged [JOIN])
- [ ] No invented scenes, dialogue or details
- [ ] World-building from later chapters moved forward where needed
- [ ] Compression map produced and saved before drafting
- [ ] Inciting incident arrives by Ch 3–4
- [ ] All [WORLD GAP] and [JOIN] flags documented for author review
